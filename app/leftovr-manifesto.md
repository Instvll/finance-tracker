# The leftovr Manifesto

> **leftovr doesn’t help people manage money. It helps people understand where they stand.**

## Why leftovr Exists

Managing money should not feel intimidating.

For many people, financial stress does not come from complicated investments or accounting. It comes from not knowing where they stand today.

Questions like:

- Can I afford this?
- What is due before payday?
- How much money is actually mine to spend?
- Did I already pay that bill?

These questions create unnecessary mental weight.

**leftovr exists to remove that uncertainty.**

It transforms scattered balances, bills, and due dates into one clear picture that helps people feel informed, prepared, and in control.

Not by overwhelming them with charts.

Not by asking them to become financial experts.

Simply by helping them answer the next important question.

---

## Our Mission

> **Help people feel calm and confident about their money every single day.**

Money is emotional.

leftovr should never add to that stress.

Every screen should reduce uncertainty.

Every interaction should build confidence.

Every update should make planning feel a little easier.

---

## Our Vision

We believe personal finance software can feel different.

Not corporate.

Not flashy.

Not overwhelming.

Instead:

- Professional
- Approachable
- Thoughtful
- Quietly beautiful

leftovr should become a product people trust because it consistently helps them understand where they stand.

---

## Our Design Philosophy

Good design is not decoration.

Good design removes work from the user.

Every element on the screen should exist for a reason.

If something does not improve clarity, it does not belong.

---

## What We Believe

### Clarity over complexity

People should understand their finances in seconds, not after exploring menus.

### Confidence over anxiety

The app should reduce financial stress, never increase it.

### Planning over reacting

Good planning creates peace of mind.

leftovr should encourage looking ahead rather than scrambling behind.

### Calm over clutter

Whitespace is valuable.

Hierarchy is valuable.

Simplicity is valuable.

Visual noise is not.

### Consistency builds trust

When every page behaves the same way, users stop thinking about the interface and simply use it.

### Small improvements matter

leftovr will not chase trends.

It will improve steadily.

Every update should leave the product a little clearer, a little smoother, and a little more enjoyable than before.

---

## The Feeling We Are Designing

When someone opens leftovr, we want them to feel:

> **“Okay. I know exactly where I stand.”**

Not:

> “Where do I start?”

Not:

> “This is overwhelming.”

Not:

> “I hope I do not miss something.”

Instead:

- Confidence
- Relief
- Control

---

## Design Principles

### Every screen should answer one primary question

**Dashboard**

> Where do I stand today?

**Bills**

> What needs my attention before payday?

**Credit Cards**

> How healthy is my credit right now?

**Editor**

> What information powers my plan?

**Settings**

> How can leftovr work better for me?

If a page tries to answer multiple unrelated questions, it should probably be simplified.

### Information should reveal itself naturally

Important information earns visual emphasis.

Supporting information stays quiet.

Nothing should compete with everything else.

### Motion should reassure

Animations exist to communicate continuity.

Not to entertain.

Not to distract.

### Every interaction should reduce uncertainty

Saving.

Editing.

Paying a bill.

Undoing a payment.

Backing up data.

Each interaction should leave the user feeling more confident than they were before.

---

## Our Product Values

leftovr is not trying to become a bank.

It is not trying to replace accounting software.

It is not trying to overwhelm users with graphs and financial jargon.

It exists to make everyday money planning easier.

If a feature does not make everyday planning simpler, it probably does not belong.

---

## The leftovr Standard

Before every release, we should be able to answer **yes** to these questions:

- Does this make someone’s finances easier to understand?
- Does this reduce mental effort?
- Does this feel calm?
- Does this feel trustworthy?
- Does this respect the user’s time?
- Would we still be proud of this feature two years from now?

If the answer to any of those questions is no, we keep refining.

---

## The Promise

Every version of leftovr should move toward the same goal:

> **Helping people spend less time worrying about their money and more time living their lives.**
